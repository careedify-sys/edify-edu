SSODL Institutional Structure Clarification

The institutional relationship between SSODL and parent Symbiosis International Deemed University Pune is worth clarifying because competitor sources often confuse this with SCDL legacy. Three structural facts.

Fact 1: SSODL is direct branch of Symbiosis International Deemed University

SSODL was established in 2023 as the dedicated online learning division of Symbiosis International (Deemed University) Pune. The institutional structure is direct: parent university is Symbiosis International Deemed University, and SSODL is the online learning branch. SSODL operates under the same NAAC A++ accreditation, UGC Category-1 status, and AICTE recognition as the parent university.

Fact 2: SSODL is not connected to SCDL

SCDL (Symbiosis Centre for Distance Learning) is a separate entity that operated traditional distance learning programmes. SCDL is not connected to SSODL and SSODL does not flow from SCDL legacy. Several competitor reviews incorrectly conflate these two; the correct understanding is that SSODL is a direct branch of Symbiosis International Deemed University, established 2023, with no operational continuity from SCDL.

Fact 3: SSODL programmes share Symbiosis institutional legacy

SSODL programmes including Online BBA, BCA, BSc, MBA, MSc, MA share the institutional legacy of Symbiosis International Deemed University. NAAC A++ accreditation, UGC Category-1 status, NIRF University rank #19 apply to SSODL programmes through parent university accreditation. The institutional credentialling delivers Tier 1 Premium brand recall identical to parent university programmes.

Symbiosis Online BBA Examination Framework

SSODL Online BBA follows structured examination framework worth understanding before enrolling.

30 internal plus 70 external component split. Each course examination has 30 percent internal component (assignments, projects, internal assessment tests) and 70 percent external university-conducted examination component. Students must pass both internal and external separately with minimum 40 percent absolute marks; failure in either component results in FAIL grade for the course requiring re-examination.

10-point Grade Point system. Maximum GP is 10 corresponding to O (Outstanding); minimum passing GP is 4 corresponding to P (Pass). The CGPA system aligns with NEP 2020 framework and supports credit portability through ABC (Academic Bank of Credits) accounts.

Minimum CGPA 4.00 for degree award. University awards BBA degree at end of semester 6 to students achieving minimum CGPA 4.00 out of 10.00 across all 6 semesters. The degree certificate is issued through Symbiosis International Deemed University with SSODL programme designation. Re-examination available for students failing to meet minimum CGPA threshold.
