### Page 1: How to Choose an Online University

**Page title:** How to Choose an Online University in India

**Content (paste exactly):**
```
Choosing an online university in India requires checking three things before anything else: UGC-DEB entitlement, NAAC accreditation, and NIRF ranking.

1. UGC-DEB Entitlement
The University Grants Commission's Distance Education Bureau (deb.ugc.ac.in) publishes an annual list of universities entitled to offer online programmes. If a university is not on this list, its online degree may not be recognized by employers or government bodies.

Check the full UGC-DEB entitled list at: https://deb.ugc.ac.in

2. NAAC Accreditation
NAAC grades universities on a scale from A++ (highest) to C (lowest). For online programmes, aim for universities with NAAC A+ or A++ grades. A higher NAAC grade signals better infrastructure, faculty quality, and learning outcomes.

3. NIRF Ranking
The National Institutional Ranking Framework (nirfindia.org) ranks universities annually. Check both the "University" and "Management" category rankings for MBA programmes.

4. Fee Transparency
Compare total programme fees, not just per-semester costs. Look for EMI options and verify fees on the official university portal before making any payment.

EdifyEdu (https://edifyedu.in) compares all these data points across 50+ online universities in one place. Use the comparison tool at https://edifyedu.in/compare to evaluate your shortlisted universities side by side.
```
