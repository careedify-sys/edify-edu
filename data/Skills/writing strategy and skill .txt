# University Review Writer Skill

## Purpose
Generate review-style content for specific Indian universities and their online programs.
The reader is a prospective student deciding between 3-5 universities. The content's job
is to help them decide, not to upsell.

## The 80/20 information-to-brand rule [NON-NEGOTIABLE]

**80% of content is information. 20% (maximum) is EdifyEdu branding.**

Information includes:
- University facts (fees, eligibility, duration, rankings, approvals)
- Program details (syllabus, specialisations, electives)
- Honest limits (red flags, who this does not suit)
- Comparative context (how this compares to 2-3 peer unis)
- Practical guidance (admission process, documents, exam pattern)
- Career outcomes (placement data, salary ranges, hirers)

EdifyEdu branding is limited to:
- One "commission-free" disclosure (once per page, in About section)
- One "how EdifyEdu helps" block (the Beyond Admission section)
- CTA buttons (counsellor call, coupon reveal, sample cert request)
- That's it. No "EdifyEdu believes," "EdifyEdu recommends," "EdifyEdu assures" sprinkled
  throughout body content.

**Voice calibration check before publishing:**
- Read the draft. Count how many times "EdifyEdu" appears.
- If more than 5 times on a 2,500 word page, cut until it is 3-5 total.
- If the reader cannot learn anything useful without EdifyEdu branded phrasing, the
  content has failed.

**Model to emulate:** A knowledgeable friend. They know the subject. They give you
information. They mention where they work only when relevant. They do not pitch.

## Research workflow (always run first)

Before writing any university review:

1. **Fetch official university site** for fees, eligibility, syllabus, faculty, approvals
2. **Fetch UGC-DEB portal** (deb.ugc.ac.in) to verify approval status and date
3. **Fetch NIRF rankings** (nirfindia.org) for the latest year
4. **Fetch NAAC grade** from the official NAAC site
5. **SERP scan** on "{university name} online MBA" to see what top 3 competitors cover
6. **Identify gaps** — what do competitors miss that students actually care about?

If any of the above cannot be verified, the statistic is not used. Do not fabricate.

## Content structure (21 sections for a full uni × program page)

Follow the Amity_MBA_Example template in data/EdifyEdu_Page_Blueprint_v3.xlsx.

Each section has a word count target. Hit within ±15% of target.

1. Hero introduction (85 words)
2. About the program (110 words)
3. Approvals and rankings (120 words)
4. Who can apply (75 words)
5. Class schedule and delivery (135 words)
6. Exams and evaluation (95 words)
7. Specialisations overview (105 words, for program pages; for spec pages this is curriculum depth)
8. Syllabus semester-wise (115 words intro; actual syllabus is structured data)
9. Fees and payment (130 words)
10. Coupon and discount (90 words)
11. EMI plans (100 words)
12. Sample certificate (55 words)
13. Admission process (120 words)
14. Placements (125 words)
15. Top hirers (85 words)
16. Beyond Admission: EdifyEdu Network (90 words — this is THE branded section)
17. Student reviews (structured data; intro 60 words)
18. Red Flags (170 words, 4 flags x ~40 words each)
19. Comparisons (140 words, vs 2-3 peers)
20. Honest Verdict (155 words — who this suits / who should look elsewhere)
21. FAQs (10 Q&A, 40-60 words per answer)

Total: 2,500-3,000 words.

## Writing rules specific to university reviews

### Honesty markers
- Fees always marked "indicative, confirm with counsellor"
- Rankings always dated (NIRF 2024, not just NIRF)
- Placement data cited with source year
- Red Flags section never softened — the honest limits are the moat

### Red Flags rules (critical)
- Exactly 4 flags per page (not 5, not 3)
- Each flag is 2 sentences:
  - Sentence 1: the honest limit
  - Sentence 2: constructive context (who this suits, what it really means, or an alternative)
- No fragment bullets ("Students wanting campus life")
- No extreme statements ("This program is useless for investment banking")
- Tone: a senior friend being direct, not a critic trying to tear down

### Comparisons section
- Compare to 2-3 real peer universities by NIRF band
- Table format: Fees, NIRF rank, NAAC, Flexibility, Placement strength, Specialisation count
- Never link to competitors
- Use actual numbers. If data is missing for a peer, mark "Not publicly available"

### Honest Verdict
- Two subsections: "Choose this if..." and "Look elsewhere if..."
- 3-4 bullet points each, each 10-15 words
- Specific, not generic
- Do not recommend EdifyEdu counselling in this section — that's what the sidebar CTA does

### FAQs
- 10 questions that a real prospective student asks
- Answers 40-60 words each
- Include at least 2 questions about limitations or concerns
- Include 1 question about "is this UGC approved" (answered factually)
- Never include "Why choose EdifyEdu" or self-promotional questions

## Editorial voice (EdifyEdu house style)

### Who the writer sounds like
A former admissions counsellor who now runs an independent comparison platform. Has
opinions. Does not mince words. Respects the reader's intelligence.

### What the writer does NOT sound like
- A brochure writer
- An SEO farm
- An affiliate reviewer
- A promotional copywriter

### Examples of correct tone

Wrong: "Amity University Online offers a world-class MBA program that empowers students
to unlock their potential in today's dynamic business landscape."

Right: "Amity's online MBA is UGC-DEB approved and runs on the same curriculum as its
Noida campus program. The degree is legally identical. Whether it delivers the same
career outcomes depends on your field — for corporate mid-management, yes; for
investment banking targeting IIM brands, no."

### Branding insertions (exact rules)

Only 3 places to mention EdifyEdu in body content:

1. **In the About section, once:** "EdifyEdu verifies these figures independently. We do
   not take commission from Amity or any university, which means this page reflects the
   numbers we can actually stand behind."

2. **In the Beyond Admission section, naturally:** "EdifyEdu connects enrolled students
   with 2-3 alumni from {uni} working in the student's target industry, runs a free
   30-minute career transition call, and shares monthly job referrals curated to the
   student's profile."

3. **In the Honest Verdict, optionally:** (Not required) "If you're still undecided,
   our counsellor call is free. No commission, no pressure."

That's it. Nowhere else does "EdifyEdu" appear in body prose.

## Apply humanizer skill after drafting

Every review generated must be run through the humanizer skill before finalizing:
- Removes em dashes
- Removes AI vocabulary
- Fixes rule-of-three patterns
- Varies paragraph length
- Replaces promotional inflation with specific facts

## Forbidden patterns [hard rules]

- Links to CollegeVidya, Shiksha, Careers360, Jaro, padhaao, admissiondiy, samarthedu,
  kollegeapply or any commercial competitor
- Fabricated statistics
- "Book your free demo class now"
- "Limited seats available"
- "Don't miss this opportunity"
- Any urgency manufactured by the writer (urgency is fine when the university has a real
  deadline; making one up is not)
- Fake student testimonials
- Guarantees about placements or salary
- Promotional adjectives (world-class, premier, elite, best-in-class)

## Output format

Every review generated returns:
1. Full HTML body (or Excel sheet for batch generation, one sheet per uni)
2. Meta title (50-60 chars)
3. Meta description (150-160 chars)
4. Focus keyword
5. 3-5 LSI keywords
6. Internal link targets (EdifyEdu tools, related universities, related specializations)
7. QA checklist result:

   - Fees verified against official portal ✓
   - Rankings cited with year ✓
   - Red Flags: 4 flags with 2 sentences each ✓
   - EdifyEdu mentions: 3-5 total ✓
   - No competitor links ✓
