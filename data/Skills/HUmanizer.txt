# Humanizer Skill

## Purpose
Transform AI-sounding text into writing that passes for human-authored editorial content.
Remove patterns that make readers think "this was written by a bot."

## When to apply
- After any draft generated by seo-blog-writer, university-review-writer, or general content generation
- When reviewing existing content that reads as mechanical or robotic
- Before publishing any EdifyEdu page

## Patterns to detect and fix

### 1. Em dashes and hyphen overuse
AI loves em dashes. Replace every em dash with one of:
- A period (two sentences)
- A comma with a connector (and, but, so)
- A colon when introducing a list or expansion
- Parentheses for true asides
- Nothing at all (delete the aside)

Example:
AI: "The program is comprehensive — covering finance, operations, and strategy — with a modern curriculum."
Human: "The program covers finance, operations, and strategy with a modern curriculum."

### 2. Rule of three
AI defaults to 3-item lists everywhere. Break this by:
- Using 2 items when 2 are enough
- Using 4 or 5 items when specificity demands it
- Writing prose instead of lists

Example:
AI: "Students gain knowledge, skills, and confidence."
Human: "Students gain the practical skills hiring managers actually test for."

### 3. Negative parallelism
"It's not just X, it's Y" is a pattern repeated endlessly.

Example:
AI: "This isn't just a degree, it's a career transformation."
Human: "This degree, done right, changes which roles you qualify for."

### 4. Vague attribution
AI says "studies show," "experts believe," "many agree" without citing.

Example:
AI: "Studies show online MBAs have strong ROI."
Human: "A 2024 NIRF-linked placement study found median online MBA ROI crossed breakeven in 2.4 years."

If you cannot cite, remove the claim.

### 5. AI vocabulary words (kill list)
Replace these words and phrases on sight:

- "delve into" → explore, look at, examine
- "robust" → strong, solid, tested
- "leverage" → use
- "streamline" → simplify
- "unlock" → get, open
- "seamless" → smooth, easy, connected
- "empower" → enable, help, give tools to
- "cutting-edge" → new, current
- "revolutionary" → significant, meaningful (or delete)
- "navigate the landscape" → find the right fit
- "in the realm of" → in
- "a plethora of" → many, several
- "myriad" → many
- "tapestry" → delete the whole sentence
- "journey" (metaphorical) → path, process, or delete
- "ecosystem" (non-tech contexts) → network, system

### 6. Superficial -ing analyses
AI uses "analyzing," "exploring," "understanding" as filler.

Example:
AI: "Understanding the fee structure helps students make informed decisions."
Human: "Know the fee structure before you enroll."

### 7. Promotional inflation
AI writes like a brochure.

Example:
AI: "This world-class program offers unparalleled opportunities for career advancement."
Human: "This program opens doors in mid-level corporate roles across 60+ recruiters."

### 8. Conjunctive overload
AI overuses "however," "moreover," "furthermore," "additionally."

Rules:
- "However" max once per 500 words
- "Moreover" never
- "Furthermore" never
- "Additionally" rarely, prefer "also" or just starting the new sentence

### 9. Symbolic inflation
AI ends paragraphs with ominous or grand proclamations.

Example:
AI: "In the ever-evolving landscape of online education, only the prepared will thrive."
Human: (Delete. Replace with a concrete next step.)

### 10. Safe hedging everywhere
AI never commits. "It might be," "could potentially," "may possibly" stack up.

Rules:
- State facts as facts
- Only hedge when genuinely uncertain
- Remove double-hedging ("might potentially")

### 11. Perfect paragraph lengths
AI writes paragraphs that are all 3-4 sentences. Vary this.

- Short punch paragraphs of 1 sentence
- Medium paragraphs of 3-5 sentences
- Occasional longer paragraphs of 6-8 sentences when explaining something complex

### 12. Introducing with meta-commentary
AI often announces what it's about to say.

Example:
AI: "Let's now look at the admission process. The admission process has several steps."
Human: "The admission process has several steps."

## Voice calibration for EdifyEdu

When humanizing EdifyEdu content specifically:

- Founder voice: Rishi writes from the perspective of someone who has looked at 130+ universities and has opinions
- Honest limits stated clearly (this is Edify's moat)
- Contrarian when the market is wrong (e.g., "most online MBA rankings are paid; ignore them")
- Specific over general (name the uni, the fee, the specialization)
- No false humility ("we might be able to help") and no false authority ("we are the leading platform")

## Output

When humanizing, return:
1. The humanized text
2. A brief list of patterns fixed (e.g., "Removed 7 em dashes, 2 rule-of-three lists, 3 AI vocab words")
3. Any content that was cut entirely (with reason)

## What not to strip

Humanizer does NOT remove:
- Technical accuracy
- SEO-relevant keywords (keep them in the natural text)
- Specific data points or numbers
- Internal links with descriptive anchor text
- The core argument of the piece

The goal is voice, not erasure.
